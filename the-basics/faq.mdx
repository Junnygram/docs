 <Accordion title="1. What is TMDB's API?">
    This API service is for person interested in using our movie, TV show or actor images and/or data in your application. 
    Our API is a system we provide for you and your team to programmatically fetch and use our data and/or images.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="2. How do I Get an API key?">
    You can get an your API key by clicking the [API](https://www.themoviedb.org/settings/api) feature from the left hand sidebar within your account settings page.
    <Frame><img height="200" noZoom src="../images/annotely_image.png" /></Frame>
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="3. Is there an SLA?">
    We do not currently provide an SLA. However, we do make every reasonable attempt to keep our service online and accessible. 
     You can view our status page [here](https://status.themoviedb.org/).
 </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="4. What about SSL?">
    It's currently available API wide. This includes both the API endpoints and assets served via our CDN. We strongly recommend you use SSL.
  </Accordion>
   <Accordion title="5. Is there a commercial service">
     Yes, by sending an email to our [sales team](mailto:sales@themoviedb.org).
    <Info>Please include your country to help us expedite your request.</Info>
   </Accordion>

   <Accordion title="6. What is the difference between a commercial API and a developer API?">
    - A <b>commercial</b> API is for commercial projects.
    - A <b>developer</b> API is for developers either for testing or building personal project(porfolio).
    
    Your project is considered commercial if the primary purpose is to create revenue for the benefit of the owner.
   </Accordion>

   <Accordion title="7. Does the API key cost anything?">
    Our API is free to use for non-commercial purposes as long as you attribute TMDB as the source of the data and/or images.
    If you are interested in obtaining a license to use our API and/or our data/images for commercial purposes, please contact our our [sales team](mailto:sales@themoviedb.org).
    <Info>Please include your country to help us expedite your request.</Info>
   </Accordion>
   <Accordion title="8. What are the attribution requirements?">
    You shall use the TMDB logo to identify your use of the TMDB APIs. You shall place the following notice prominently on your application: 
    <Card>This product uses the TMDB API but is not endorsed or certified by TMDB.</Card>
    
    Any use of the TMDB logo in your application shall be less prominent than the logo or mark that primarily describes the application and your use of the TMDB logo shall not imply any endorsement by TMDB. When attributing TMDB, the attribution must be within your application's "About" or "Credits" type section.

    <Note>When using a TMDB logo, we require you to use [one of our approved logos](https://www.themoviedb.org/about/logos-attribution).</Note>
   </Accordion>
<Accordion title="9. Can I make changes to the API?">
    No, you cannot. Our API is closed source.   
</Accordion>

   <Accordion title=" 10. Are there branding requirements?">
    Our logo should not be modified in color, aspect ratio, flipped or rotated except where otherwise noted.

    Our logo can be white, black or any of the approved colors used throughout our branding. For a list of official logos, see [our logos & attribution page](https://www.themoviedb.org/about/logos-attribution).

    When referring to TMDB, you should use either the acronym <b>TMDB</b> or the full name <b>The Movie Database</b>. Any other name is not acceptable. When linking back to our website, please point to our [website](https://www.themoviedb.org/).
   </Accordion>

    <Warning>If you are putting our company name or logo on any merchandise or product packaging, please consult us beforehand for approval.</Warning>
